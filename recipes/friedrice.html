Fried Rice

Description

Fried rice is a popular dish made by stir-frying cooked rice with a variety of ingredients, such as vegetables, eggs, meat, or seafood, in a hot wok or skillet. Originating in China, it has become a versatile and widely enjoyed dish across the world, with countless regional variations.

The key to good fried rice is ensuring that the rice is dry enough to achieve a slightly crispy texture, and it's often seasoned with soy sauce, oyster sauce, or other flavorful condiments. The dish is typically quick to prepare, making it a favorite for using up leftovers or as a simple, satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 2/3 cup chopped baby carrots
  • 1/2 cup frozen green peas
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 cups leftover cooked and chilled white rice
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce, or more to taste
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil, or to taste
  • 1 clove garlic, minced, or to taste (Optional)

Steps

  1. Step 1: Assemble ingredients.
  2. Step 2: Place carrots in a small saucepan and cover with water. Bring to a low boil and cook for 3 to 5 minutes. Stir in peas, then immediately drain in a colander.
  3. Step 3: Heat a wok over high heat. Pour in vegetable oil, then stir in carrots, peas, and garlic; cook for about 30 seconds. Add eggs; stir quickly to scramble eggs with vegetables.
  4. Step 4: Stir in cooked rice. Add soy sauce and toss rice to coat. Drizzle with sesame oil and toss again.
  5. Step 5: Serve hot and enjoy!
